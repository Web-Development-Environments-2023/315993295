    • About me

    Hey, I'm Ozzy Osbourne, the Prince of Darkness! I've been rockin' since the late 1960s, and I'm still going strong today. I'm known for my wild stage antics, my distinctive vocals, and my unique sense of humor. I've had my share of ups and downs over the years, but I've always stayed true to my passion for music and my fans.

    I started out with Black Sabbath, and we changed the face of rock with our dark, heavy sound. But when I went solo, I got to really explore my own artistic vision. I've released some classic albums, like "Blizzard of Ozz," "Diary of a Madman," and "No More Tears." I've had some crazy experiences along the way, like biting the head off a bat on stage (it was an accident, I swear!) and getting fired from Black Sabbath for being too out of control.

    Despite all the craziness, though, I'm proud of my legacy and my impact on rock music.

    Difference Web 1.0 Web 2.0 Web 3.0
    Coined in 1996 2006 2016
    Number of users Millions of users Billions of users Trillions of users
    Communication / Media Pushed web, text/graphics based flash Two ways web pages, wikis, videos, pod casts, shading, Personal publishing, 2D portals. 3D portals, avatar representation, Interoperable profits, Multi-user virtual environment (MUVEs), Integrated games, education and business, all media flows in and out of virtual web worlds.

    Let's look at an example of searching to compare Web 1.0 and Web 3.0:

    In Web 1.0 search engines retrieve macro contents. Search is very fast but many times results are inaccurate or more than users can chew.
    These websites would be static and would provide information in a one-way manner, with no interaction or engagement possible beyond clicking on links to other pages.

    In Web 3.0 search engines will hopefully retrieve micro content texts which were tagged automatically. This implies translating billions of Web 1.0 macro contents into micro contents. The result could be more precise search because tagging can solve part of the ambiguity that homonyms and synonyms introduce into the process of Search.
    you might use a next-generation search engine that employs natural language processing and semantic web technologies to understand your query and deliver more personalized and relevant results. These search engines could potentially provide you with not only text-based results but also audio, video, and interactive content, as well as personalized recommendations based on your search history and other data.

    * Based on the research paper: "International Journal of Advanced Research in Computer Science and Software Engineering".
